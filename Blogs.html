Martial Arts - A Better Option Towards a Better Self

by cdt. Vibhuti Sharma

Martial arts not only train the mind and body for strength and stamina, but it also aids the body in fighting diseases, remaining flexible, and active. It improves patience and mental stability, allowing you to make better decisions in difficult situations. Martial arts, in addition to improving reflexes and concentration, can aid in weight loss and fitness... read more → There are traces of various martial arts in Indian history that describe the use of spears, swords, shields, and bows. Martial arts were not an optional extracurricular activity for our forefathers, but rather a required part of their daily routine. After years of practice and improvement, Martial Arts eventually became a part of the culture. People in India used to be very fit and healthy due to the high physical work interactions they had in their daily lives, but technological advancement has had a significant impact. People nowadays are more focused on mental and static work, which not only causes stress and mental imbalance but also diverts people from many physical activities. This is why young people should take care of their bodies, get active, and get their bodies back into shape. Martial arts are a fusion of yoga, aerobics, sports, and a variety of other physical exercises in a specifically designed format for attack and defence. Martial arts training offers numerous physical benefits as well as the ability to defend oneself. Karate is a type of martial art that focuses on making people physically strong as well as mentally calm when it comes to self-defence. Self-defence allows one to be self-sufficient and is a valuable skill that everyone should learn. Martial arts' intentions are not immorality or emotionlessness, but to promote determination, perseverance, consistency, and discipline, as well as to strengthen individuals to fight for good and help the weak. Martial arts, more than sport, fighting, or self-defence, is about knowing the essence and expression of life, as Bruce Lee once said, "To be a martial artist means to be an artist of life." Martial arts have the ability to instil self-confidence and respect in people. Citizens of a developing country, such as India, should be healthy, active, and, above all, happy. Thus, martial arts are a great option because it contributes to an individual's physical and mental development while also preserving the nation's intangible cultural heritage. Read Less →

Rules & Regulations

Training rules of DOJO:-

1. Bow in and place shoes next to the door of the Dojo Hall; no shoes on the training floor.
2. Cadets should wear karate uniform in the class daily.
3. The uniform must be clean, odor free and in good repair.
4. Cadets must not do any other activity except training while wearing the uniform. read more → 5. Maintain the decorum of karate uniform by tightening the belt properly and same for untying the belt after class. 6. Maintain personal cleanliness, nails cut and hair kept neat. 7. No jewelry of any kind should be worn for safety reasons. 8. Headguard, mouthpiece, hand pads, cup / supporters must be worn at all times during sparring (fight practice – Kumite) 9. Students are responsible for any valuables they bring to the dojo and for any valuables lost or stolen. 10. Nothing should be taken as food or drink before 1 Hour of the class timing, it will be unhealthy to your Body. 11. No chewing gum, food or drinks of any kind are allowed in the workout area. 12. Maintain workout equipment and do not remove it from the dojo; cadets are not allowed to carry out the academy’s kit or equipment’s without the permission of instructor. 13. Proper record of the equipment’s must be maintained by the Cadets of Academy if the equipment’s are taken out from dojo. 14. Misuse of training and techniques of martial arts by any cadet will lead to the cancellation of the membership from the academy. Read Less →

Class Procedure

1. Show respect for the dojo and instructors by bowing in before entering and leaving the workout area.
2. Start the class formally by showing respect and bowing (rei) by saying “ous” to the national flag,dojo hall, instructor (teacher) and by saying the motivational slogan “jai hind “and finish the class by same. read more → 3. Address all instructors as “Sir” or “Sensei” 4. Conduct yourself professionally; horseplay or indiscipline are not allowed in the dojo. 5. Line up in class according to rank (with height second), from left to right. 6. Warmup, stretching and core strengthening exercise should be done daily after the start of the class. 7. Instructor will only supervise all weapon training to the students according to their class performance. 8. Instructor will supervise all sparring sessions; no “play” or informal sparring without instructor consent. 9. Cardio and weapon training would be done in the ground under the guidance of karate instructor. Read Less →
